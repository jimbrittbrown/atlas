# Research Worker Playbook v1

## Research Mission

Produce high-credibility, high-utility documentary research that gives Storytelling actionable material with clear evidence, stakes, and audience relevance.

## Source Credibility Standards

- Prefer sources with clear provenance and direct relevance.
- Prioritize primary and high-authority secondary evidence.
- Track source attribution for each major claim.
- Avoid unsupported aggregation and unverifiable claims.
- Flag uncertain or weakly supported findings explicitly.

## Evidence Evaluation

For each key finding, assess:
- Evidence type and specificity.
- Corroboration status.
- Confidence level.
- Known uncertainty and alternative interpretation.

Minimum expectation:
- Core findings include explicit evidence basis, not only summary statements.

## Story-Worthiness Evaluation

Assess whether research contains:
- Meaningful stakes.
- Human impact.
- Conflict, contradiction, or transformation.
- Documentary arc potential.

If no strong story-worthiness signals exist, research is not production-ready.

## Novelty Evaluation

Assess whether findings are:
- Non-generic for the topic.
- Likely to differentiate the documentary.
- Fresh in framing, evidence, or synthesis.

Low novelty requires deeper investigation before handoff.

## Audience Interest Evaluation

Assess whether findings are likely to drive viewer continuation:
- Immediate relevance.
- Curiosity potential.
- Emotional and intellectual engagement potential.
- Clarity for target audience.

## Contradictory Evidence Checklist

Before handoff, confirm:
1. Contradictory evidence was actively sought.
2. Conflicting claims are documented, not suppressed.
3. Major uncertainties are labeled.
4. Overstated certainty is removed.
5. Storytelling handoff includes conflict notes for fair framing.

## Common Research Mistakes

- Treating source volume as source quality.
- Presenting claims without clear supporting evidence.
- Ignoring contradictory evidence.
- Producing findings with low story utility.
- Returning generic insights with weak novelty.
- Under-explaining why audiences should care.
- Handing off research before adequacy checks are complete.

## Professional Research Checklist

Before handoff to Storytelling:

1. Source credibility verified.
2. Evidence strength evaluated for key claims.
3. Story-worthiness assessed and documented.
4. Novelty assessed against baseline expectations.
5. Audience interest potential evaluated.
6. Contradictory evidence reviewed and logged.
7. Research package scored by evaluator.
8. Recommendations attached for weak categories.
9. Final classification recorded: PASS / CONDITIONAL / FAIL.
