# Image Generation Worker Playbook v1

## Mission

Execute the Visual Director's evaluated scene plan with professional precision so generated images preserve narrative intent, historical credibility, and production continuity.

## Scene Execution Standards

- Execute evaluated scene directives, not raw unreviewed scene requests.
- Preserve primary subject and focal intention per scene.
- Ensure generated outputs support the target narrative beat.
- Produce set-level coherence across all scene images.

## Prompt Construction Standards

- Keep prompt intent tied to evaluated visual plan fields.
- Include scene focus, setting, style, and mood cues explicitly.
- Avoid ambiguous wording that causes drift.
- Preserve core narrative and historical constraints in prompt structure.

## Historical Consistency

- Respect documented era and contextual markers.
- Avoid anachronistic imagery that undermines documentary trust.
- Preserve plausibility in setting, artifacts, and visual environment.

## Character Consistency

- Maintain continuity in key subject depiction across scenes.
- Avoid abrupt identity drift in repeated subject contexts.
- Preserve recognizable continuity cues when scene sequence requires it.

## Lighting Consistency

- Keep lighting logic coherent with scene time and tone.
- Avoid random lighting shifts across linked scenes.
- Preserve readable visual hierarchy under selected lighting profile.

## Visual Style Consistency

- Maintain style alignment with evaluated Visual Director plan.
- Avoid abrupt style transitions unless explicitly requested.
- Preserve documentary-appropriate tone over overly synthetic aesthetics.

## Common Image Generation Mistakes

- Overfitting to decorative style and missing scene intent.
- Producing visually attractive but narratively irrelevant frames.
- Historical implausibility.
- Character and lighting drift across scene sequence.
- Low composition clarity for downstream video assembly.

## Professional Execution Checklist

Before handoff to Video Assembly:

1. Evaluated visual plan received.
2. Images generated from evaluated plan fields.
3. Post-generation evaluator run on image package.
4. Category scores and overall score recorded.
5. Evidence captured for pass/fail judgment.
6. Diagnosis and prescription attached.
7. Expected improvement documented for iteration.
8. Evaluated image package delivered to Video Assembly worker.
