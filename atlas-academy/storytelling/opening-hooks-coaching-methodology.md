# Module 1: Opening Hooks Coaching Methodology

## Coaching Principle
Use deliberate practice loops that isolate one weakness at a time and enforce measurable improvement before broad integration.

## Coaching Inputs
- Ranked hook evaluations
- Recurring weakness frequencies
- Best exemplar hook pattern

## Coaching Output Structure
1. Focus weakness
- Highest-frequency weakness from current cycle.

2. Exemplar pattern
- Pattern extracted from top-ranked hook to preserve strengths.

3. Deliberate drills
- Targeted exercises tied to weakness category.

4. Next-cycle instruction
- Explicit instruction for next generation loop.

## Drill Library
### Opening Strength drills
- Rewrite first sentence 5 times with consequence-first framing.
- Remove setup phrases that delay stakes.
- Add specific actor + decision + consequence in line one.

### Curiosity drills
- Create 5 unresolved accountability questions.
- Keep only questions that imply non-obvious consequence.
- Delay direct reveal language until after curiosity pressure is established.

### Audience Commitment drills
- Add continuation reason tied to concrete stakes.
- Ensure opening promise is specific, not generic suspense.
- End hook with unresolved but credible forward pressure.

## Coaching Progression
- Corrective stage: frequent drill assignment.
- Consolidation stage: fewer drills, higher precision.
- Maintenance stage: exemplar preservation and variation checks.

## Success Signal
Coaching is considered effective when recurring weakness frequency drops and elite-level hook scores remain stable across consecutive cycles.
