# Visual Director Playbook v1

## Visual Storytelling Mission

Translate documentary narrative intent into historically credible, emotionally effective, and cinematically coherent visual scenes that increase audience trust and continuation.

## Scene Selection Principles

- Select scenes with clear narrative function.
- Prefer scenes that reveal stakes, conflict, or turning points.
- Avoid decorative scenes with low informational or emotional value.
- Ensure each selected scene advances understanding.

## Historical Accuracy Standards

- Verify era, location, and artifact plausibility.
- Avoid anachronistic props, architecture, or attire cues.
- Reflect known documentary context in scene framing.
- Flag uncertain historical assumptions explicitly.

## Narrative Support Checklist

1. What narrative beat does this scene support?
2. Is the scene aligned with script meaning and timing?
3. Does it reinforce stakes or consequences?
4. Is this scene necessary for comprehension or momentum?
5. Would removing this scene reduce story clarity?

## Emotional Reinforcement

- Match emotional tone to documentary intent.
- Use lighting, scale, and framing to reinforce tension or empathy.
- Avoid emotional overstatement that damages credibility.
- Keep emotion grounded in evidence and narrative truth.

## Cinematic Composition

- Use focal hierarchy to direct attention to key story elements.
- Preserve compositional clarity under fast viewer scanning.
- Balance atmosphere with readability.
- Keep visual complexity proportional to narrative need.

## Visual Continuity

- Maintain consistent visual language across adjacent scenes.
- Track continuity of location, time, and character portrayal cues.
- Prevent abrupt style drift unless narratively justified.
- Ensure transition logic supports narrative flow.

## Common Mistakes

- Visually impressive but narratively irrelevant scenes.
- Historical inaccuracies that reduce trust.
- Tone mismatch between script and visual treatment.
- Continuity breaks across scene sequence.
- Over-stylization that obscures documentary clarity.
- Repetitive scene plans with low progression value.

## Professional Checklist

Before image generation:

1. Visual plan scored by evaluator.
2. Story relevance validated for each scene.
3. Historical risks identified and mitigated.
4. Emotional target and cinematic intent documented.
5. Continuity risks reviewed.
6. Recommendations incorporated for low-scoring categories.
7. Final classification recorded: PASS / CONDITIONAL / FAIL.
