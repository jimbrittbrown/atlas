# Narration Director Playbook v1

## Narration Mission

Deliver documentary narration that is clear, emotionally accurate, and rhythmically controlled so audiences remain engaged and informed throughout the narrative.

## Natural Pacing

- Keep pacing aligned with content complexity.
- Slow down for dense facts, names, and transitions.
- Increase tempo only when narrative momentum requires it.
- Avoid monotone speed across the full script.

## Strategic Pauses

- Use pauses before high-stakes statements.
- Pause after key claims to improve retention.
- Use micro-pauses at complex sentence boundaries.
- Avoid excessive pause frequency that disrupts flow.

## Emotional Emphasis

- Emphasize words that carry consequence and meaning.
- Match intensity to evidence strength.
- Avoid exaggerated drama that undermines trust.
- Keep emotional expression credible and restrained where appropriate.

## Tone Consistency

- Maintain a consistent vocal identity across segments.
- Ensure tone changes are intentional and tied to narrative shifts.
- Avoid abrupt tonal jumps without contextual reason.

## Listener Engagement

- Vary cadence and stress patterns to avoid flat delivery.
- Use directional phrasing to keep forward momentum.
- Reinforce continuation cues at key transitions.

## Clarity Standards

- Prioritize articulation over speed.
- Keep sentence stress aligned to semantic structure.
- Reduce mumbling risk on dense lines.
- Preserve intelligibility under different playback conditions.

## Pronunciation Guidance

- Pre-mark difficult names and terms.
- Use pronunciation notes for multilingual or technical vocabulary.
- Standardize recurring proper nouns across the script.
- Rehearse high-risk terms before final generation.

## Common Narration Mistakes

- Flat delivery with no emotional contour.
- Overly dramatic performance that reduces credibility.
- Rushed pacing through critical evidence.
- Inconsistent tone between adjacent segments.
- Weak articulation on names and key terms.
- Pause placement that interrupts meaning.

## Professional Narration Checklist

Before voice generation:

1. Narration plan evaluated with category scores.
2. Natural flow and pacing risks reviewed.
3. Strategic pause points identified.
4. Emotional emphasis mapped to narrative stakes.
5. Tone consistency verified across segments.
6. Clarity and pronunciation risks addressed.
7. Recommendations applied for low-scoring categories.
8. Final classification recorded: PASS / CONDITIONAL / FAIL.
