# Atlas Web Visual Reference Brief

## Visual Objective
Create an original, premium visual experience that signals commercial credibility and conversion intent immediately.

## Core Visual Language
- Premium editorial composition
- Strong typography hierarchy
- Sophisticated spacing rhythm
- Controlled, purposeful motion
- Clear transformation storytelling
- Business credibility over startup-tech aesthetics

## Layout Rhythm
- Alternate dense proof sections with breathing whitespace sections.
- Keep first screen high impact and low clutter.
- Use predictable vertical rhythm for readability and trust.

## Typography Roles
- Display: high-impact headlines for value proposition and proof framing.
- Heading: section-level clarity and scannability.
- Body: concise, plain-language commercial messaging.
- Caption/meta: disclosures, context labels, and supportive notes.

## Color Roles
- Primary: confidence base (deep neutral tones).
- Accent: conversion action emphasis (single controlled accent family).
- Support neutrals: readability and section separation.
- Status colors: validation/error states for forms and accessibility clarity.

## Image Treatment
- High-quality, intentional imagery only.
- Avoid generic stock clichés and overused AI motifs.
- Ensure images support conversion narrative, not decoration.

## Before/After Presentation
- Use clear side-by-side or slider comparison with fixed labels.
- Keep baseline scale and framing equivalent across states.
- Pair each visual with concise improvement rationale and disclosure.

## Card System
- Strong hierarchy: title, short outcome, disclosure/status chip.
- Consistent radius, spacing, and elevation language.
- Avoid crowded card layouts and excessive ornament.

## Forms
- Clean, compact, and confidence-building.
- Strong field labels and clear helper text.
- High-contrast controls and obvious submit state.

## Buttons
- Primary button style consistent across pages.
- Secondary style subdued but clear.
- Maintain visible hover/focus/active states.

## Motion
- Use subtle transitions for clarity, not spectacle.
- Motion should guide attention to proof and CTA.
- Keep timing fast and non-distracting.

## Responsive Behavior
- Mobile-first readability and tap targets.
- Preserve proof prominence and CTA continuity on small screens.
- Prevent dense blocks and side-scrolling comparisons.

## Accessibility
- Maintain adequate contrast ratios.
- Provide keyboard-focus visibility.
- Ensure logical reading order and heading structure.
- Include descriptive alt text and form error guidance.

## Visual Restraint Rules
- Avoid trend-driven gimmicks.
- Avoid over-animation.
- Avoid decorative complexity that reduces clarity.
- Prioritize confidence, credibility, and conversion performance.

## Provider-Neutral Constraint
- Do not encode provider-specific components, frameworks, widgets, or platform conventions.
