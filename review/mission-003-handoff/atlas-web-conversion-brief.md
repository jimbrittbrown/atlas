# Atlas Web Conversion Brief

## Primary Conversion Goal
- Generate qualified submissions for the Free Website Audit.

## Secondary Conversion Goals
- Drive engagement with Demonstration Projects.
- Increase trust interactions (proof views, process section views, contact interactions).
- Capture enough qualification context for useful follow-up.

## CTA Hierarchy
1. Primary CTA: Request Your Free Website Audit.
2. Secondary CTA: View Demonstration Projects.
3. Tertiary CTA: Contact Atlas Web.

## Form Strategy
- Keep the form short, clear, and business-relevant.
- Collect only fields needed for qualification and first response quality.
- Avoid long multi-step forms unless progressive disclosure is required on mobile.

## Recommended Lead Qualification Fields
- Name
- Business name
- Business website URL
- Email
- Phone (optional or required per CEO decision)
- Primary service area/city
- What outcome matters most? (qualified leads, booked opportunities, conversion rate)
- Current website challenge (short text)

## Friction Controls
- One primary CTA language sitewide.
- Limit required fields to essentials.
- Use clear labels and inline validation.
- Show no-obligation statement near submit action.

## Trust Placement
- Place trust language near hero, form, and final CTA.
- Include demonstration disclosure near all proof modules.
- Include privacy statement access at every form entry point.

## Proof Placement
- Immediate before/after proof above the fold.
- Demonstration previews before deep process explanation.
- Evidence-backed principle summaries near proof modules.

## Confirmation Experience
- Immediate on-submit confirmation message.
- Set expectation for response timing (pending CEO final SLA decision).
- Reinforce no-obligation and no-guarantee policy.

## Analytics Events (Readiness Specification)
- page_view_home
- click_primary_cta_hero
- open_before_after_module
- click_demo_project_card
- start_audit_form
- submit_audit_form
- submit_audit_form_error
- view_process_section
- click_contact_cta

## Conversion Risks
- Generic visual design that weakens confidence.
- Proof modules not prominent enough on first screen.
- Overly technical or AI-centric messaging.
- Inconsistent CTA wording.
- Long form completion friction.
- Missing disclosure on demonstrations.
- Implied guarantees or unsupported claims.
