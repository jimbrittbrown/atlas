# Atlas Web Page Blueprints

## A. Home

### Section 1: Navigation
- Purpose: orient quickly and set confidence.
- Visitor question answered: Where do I go first?
- Recommended content: Home, Demonstration Projects, Free Website Audit, Our Process, Contact.
- Recommended visual treatment: minimal premium top bar, persistent CTA button.
- CTA behavior: sticky primary CTA visible at all times on desktop and mobile.
- Required trust safeguards: no inflated claims in nav labels.
- Mobile behavior: compact menu with first action as CTA.

### Section 2: Visually Exceptional Hero
- Purpose: communicate what Atlas Web does immediately.
- Visitor question answered: What is this and why should I care?
- Recommended content: one-sentence value proposition focused on qualified leads and booked opportunities.
- Recommended visual treatment: high-contrast headline, premium composition, clean supporting line.
- CTA behavior: primary button only: Request Your Free Website Audit.
- Required trust safeguards: avoid unverified proof language.
- Mobile behavior: headline wraps cleanly within 2-4 lines; CTA above fold.

### Section 3: Immediate Transformation Proof
- Purpose: provide instant visual evidence.
- Visitor question answered: Can they actually improve websites?
- Recommended content: prominent before-and-after transformation module with disclosure.
- Recommended visual treatment: large split layout or slider with clear "Before" and "After" labels.
- CTA behavior: secondary in-module link to Demonstration Projects; primary CTA remains visible globally.
- Required trust safeguards: mandatory disclosure under each transformation.
- Mobile behavior: stacked before/after cards with swipe interaction optional.

### Section 4: Core Business Outcome Proposition
- Purpose: connect design work to measurable business outcomes.
- Visitor question answered: How does this help my business?
- Recommended content: 3 short outcome pillars (qualified leads, booking quality, conversion clarity).
- Recommended visual treatment: structured cards with simple icons.
- CTA behavior: small inline CTA to audit section.
- Required trust safeguards: no guaranteed outcomes.
- Mobile behavior: one-column cards with clear spacing.

### Section 5: Demonstration Project Previews
- Purpose: route visitors to proof before long explanation.
- Visitor question answered: Can I inspect examples now?
- Recommended content: preview cards for demonstration transformations.
- Recommended visual treatment: visually rich grid with consistent ratio and disclosure chips.
- CTA behavior: View Demonstration Projects.
- Required trust safeguards: each card marked Demonstration.
- Mobile behavior: vertical cards with strong thumbnail hierarchy.

### Section 6: Evidence-Backed Design Principles
- Purpose: explain recommendation logic briefly.
- Visitor question answered: Are decisions based on opinion or evidence?
- Recommended content: concise principles linking UX decisions to conversion impact.
- Recommended visual treatment: numbered principle list with short explanatory lines.
- CTA behavior: micro CTA to Free Website Audit.
- Required trust safeguards: use "evidence-backed" not "research-backed".
- Mobile behavior: collapsible list optional if readability remains high.

### Section 7: Free Website Audit Offer
- Purpose: convert high-intent visitors.
- Visitor question answered: What do I get if I submit?
- Recommended content: offer summary, no-obligation statement, short form entry point.
- Recommended visual treatment: trust-focused panel with compact form preview.
- CTA behavior: primary conversion action.
- Required trust safeguards: no guarantee language.
- Mobile behavior: simplified form start with progressive disclosure.

### Section 8: Concise Process
- Purpose: reduce uncertainty without overwhelming detail.
- Visitor question answered: What happens next?
- Recommended content: short 3-5 step process summary.
- Recommended visual treatment: horizontal timeline on desktop, stacked steps on mobile.
- CTA behavior: reinforce primary CTA after process summary.
- Required trust safeguards: no internal architecture references.
- Mobile behavior: step accordion with short copy blocks.

### Section 9: Final CTA
- Purpose: capture decision momentum.
- Visitor question answered: What should I do now?
- Recommended content: one-line prompt + primary CTA.
- Recommended visual treatment: high-contrast conversion band.
- CTA behavior: Request Your Free Website Audit.
- Required trust safeguards: no urgency manipulation.
- Mobile behavior: full-width button.

### Section 10: Footer
- Purpose: compliance and confidence.
- Visitor question answered: Is this credible and transparent?
- Recommended content: contact details, privacy link, demonstration disclosure reminder.
- Recommended visual treatment: clean, restrained legal footer.
- CTA behavior: optional repeat CTA.
- Required trust safeguards: legal details must be confirmed facts only.
- Mobile behavior: simple vertical footer blocks.

## B. Demonstration Projects
- Purpose: provide transparent proof of capability.
- Visitor question answered: What can Atlas Web do for a business like mine?
- Recommended content: curated before/after demos with concise rationale and disclosure.
- Recommended visual treatment: premium case-grid with comparison focus.
- CTA behavior: contextual CTA to free audit after each project.
- Required trust safeguards: every item disclosed as demonstration work.
- Mobile behavior: one demonstration per viewport with swipe-friendly comparison.

## C. Free Website Audit
- Purpose: convert intent into qualified lead submissions.
- Visitor question answered: Is this worth submitting and what happens next?
- Recommended content: short benefit statement, fields, no-obligation and privacy language.
- Recommended visual treatment: high-contrast form card with clear progress and low friction.
- CTA behavior: single submit action with clear confirmation state.
- Required trust safeguards: no promise of guaranteed outcomes.
- Mobile behavior: minimal fields per screen and clear input labels.

## D. Our Process
- Purpose: communicate execution discipline after proof is established.
- Visitor question answered: How does Atlas Web deliver results responsibly?
- Recommended content: concise steps from audit to implementation planning and handoff.
- Recommended visual treatment: clear step modules, minimal verbosity.
- CTA behavior: CTA after final step and in sticky header.
- Required trust safeguards: avoid internal Atlas terms and internal system references.
- Mobile behavior: stacked step cards with readable spacing.

## E. Contact
- Purpose: support trust-preserving direct communication.
- Visitor question answered: How can I reach Atlas Web and what response can I expect?
- Recommended content: channels, expected response window, short reason-for-contact prompt.
- Recommended visual treatment: simple, premium contact panel with clear hierarchy.
- CTA behavior: mirrors primary CTA and allows direct message option.
- Required trust safeguards: only confirmed contact and legal information.
- Mobile behavior: tap-friendly contact actions and concise form fields.
