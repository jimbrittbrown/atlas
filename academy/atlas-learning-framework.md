# ATLAS ACADEMY
## Atlas Learning Framework
### Version 1.0

## Purpose

The Atlas Learning Framework is the governing educational operating standard for all Atlas Academy schools. It defines how capabilities are designed, developed, evaluated, certified, and continuously improved across every domain.

This framework ensures that Atlas Academy programs are:
- Professionally rigorous.
- Operationally measurable.
- Mission-relevant.
- Ethically grounded.
- Continuously improvable.

All school-specific curricula must conform to this framework.

## 1. Educational Philosophy

Atlas Academy is built on capability-first professional education. Learning is not defined by content exposure, but by demonstrated ability under real conditions.

Educational philosophy principles:
- Competence over completion: finishing modules is insufficient without measurable capability.
- Evidence over assertion: every claim of mastery must be supported by observed performance artifacts.
- Practice before credential: credentials follow repeatable execution, not theoretical familiarity.
- Context before abstraction: learners must understand real-world conditions before optimizing methods.
- Integrity before speed: correctness, traceability, and ethics take precedence over output volume.
- Adaptation over rigidity: standards are stable, methods evolve based on mission evidence.

## 2. Capability Lifecycle

Every Atlas capability follows a full lifecycle from definition to renewal.

Lifecycle stages:
1. Define: Specify the capability, professional use case, failure consequences, and required standard.
2. Decompose: Break capability into sub-capabilities, skills, mental models, and observable behaviors.
3. Train: Deliver structured learning, guided practice, and supervised execution.
4. Validate: Evaluate against objective criteria using auditable evidence.
5. Certify: Grant capability status once minimum professional thresholds are met.
6. Deploy: Apply capability in supervised real missions.
7. Monitor: Track performance quality, drift, and reliability over time.
8. Refine: Improve curriculum and standards based on outcome data.
9. Recertify: Revalidate competency at defined intervals or after major standard updates.

No capability remains permanently certified without renewal.

## 3. Capability Maturity Model

Atlas Academy uses a five-level maturity model for all capability domains.

Level 1: Foundational Awareness
- Understands terminology, concepts, and basic workflows.
- Requires full supervision.
- Unable to perform independently under ambiguity.

Level 2: Structured Execution
- Performs bounded tasks using defined procedures.
- Produces partially reliable outputs.
- Requires supervision for exceptions and edge cases.

Level 3: Professional Competence
- Delivers reliable outcomes across routine and moderately complex scenarios.
- Applies judgment with explicit uncertainty handling.
- Requires limited supervision.

Level 4: Advanced Professional Capability
- Handles high complexity, conflicting evidence, and dynamic constraints.
- Anticipates risks and failure modes.
- Produces decision-grade outputs consistently.

Level 5: Strategic Mastery
- Shapes standards, mentors others, and leads domain strategy.
- Operates reliably in high-stakes conditions.
- Improves systems, not only individual outputs.

Default school graduation threshold: Level 3, unless explicitly raised.

## 4. Professional Competency Definition

Professional competency is the verified ability to perform domain-critical tasks to standard under realistic constraints, with repeatability, traceability, and ethical integrity.

A competency claim is valid only when all are present:
- Task performance: completes required task outcomes.
- Quality performance: meets objective quality thresholds.
- Reliability performance: sustains outcomes across repeated trials.
- Context performance: performs under realistic operational conditions.
- Accountability performance: explains reasoning, sources, and limitations.

Competency is demonstrated, not declared.

## 5. Mental Model Development

Atlas Academy requires explicit mental model formation as a core learning outcome.

Mental model development process:
1. Model Identification: Define which mental models are required for the capability.
2. Model Instruction: Teach structural logic, assumptions, and boundary conditions.
3. Model Application: Apply models in scenario-based and mission-based practice.
4. Model Stress Testing: Challenge models with conflicting evidence and edge cases.
5. Model Refinement: Update model usage based on outcomes and review.

Mental model quality criteria:
- Predictive utility: helps anticipate outcomes.
- Explanatory utility: clarifies why outcomes occurred.
- Transferability: applies across related contexts.
- Boundary awareness: explicitly states where the model fails.

## 6. Skill Decomposition Methodology

Each professional capability must be decomposed into trainable and testable units.

Required decomposition structure:
- Capability: the full professional outcome.
- Sub-capabilities: major components of execution.
- Skills: specific actions required to execute sub-capabilities.
- Micro-skills: smallest observable behaviors that can be practiced and assessed.
- Dependencies: prerequisite knowledge, tools, and mental models.
- Failure modes: common errors and risk points per skill layer.

Decomposition rules:
- Each skill must map to at least one measurable performance objective.
- Each micro-skill must have an observable behavior definition.
- No objective may rely on undeclared dependencies.
- Each critical failure mode must have a mitigation practice.

## 7. Practice Mission Methodology

Practice missions are structured simulations or supervised tasks that operationalize learning.

Practice mission design requirements:
- Mission objective: explicit professional task outcome.
- Constraints: realistic time, resource, and uncertainty conditions.
- Success criteria: objective output and quality standards.
- Evidence capture: logs, artifacts, decision records, and rationale traces.
- Reflection protocol: post-mission review of decisions, errors, and improvements.

Mission progression model:
1. Guided Mission: high scaffolding, low ambiguity.
2. Structured Mission: moderate scaffolding, moderate ambiguity.
3. Independent Mission: minimal scaffolding, high realism.
4. Adversarial Mission: conflicting signals, pressure, and edge-case complexity.

Completion without evidence capture is not valid mission completion.

## 8. Objective Evaluation Framework

Evaluation must be objective, reproducible, and auditable.

Evaluation dimensions:
- Accuracy: correctness of outputs.
- Completeness: coverage of required components.
- Quality: adherence to professional standards.
- Reasoning integrity: clarity and validity of decision logic.
- Traceability: link between conclusions and supporting evidence.
- Reliability: consistency across repeated evaluations.
- Ethical compliance: adherence to professional and safety standards.

Evaluation scoring model:
- Exceeds Standard
- Meets Standard
- Approaches Standard
- Does Not Meet Standard

Evaluation controls:
- Rubric-driven assessment.
- Multiple independent assessment points for critical competencies.
- Blind review where feasible.
- Mandatory remediation plans for substandard performance.

## 9. Graduation Criteria

Graduation from any Atlas Academy school requires all conditions below:

1. Required curriculum completion.
2. Mastery of all critical objectives at Meets Standard or above.
3. No unresolved critical ethical or integrity violations.
4. Capability maturity at school-defined threshold (minimum Level 3 unless specified).
5. Successful completion of capstone mission with full evidence package.
6. Positive final review board decision based on auditable records.

Graduation outcomes:
- Pass
- Conditional Pass (targeted remediation required)
- Fail

Conditional Pass cannot be issued if critical competency criteria are unmet.

## 10. Real Mission Validation

Capability claims must be validated beyond classroom simulation.

Real mission validation requirements:
- Application of learned capability on at least one live or production-representative mission.
- Observation of performance under genuine uncertainty and operational constraints.
- Independent evaluator confirmation of outcome quality.
- Post-mission retrospective documenting lessons and capability deltas.

Validation statuses:
- Validated: demonstrated to standard in real conditions.
- Provisionally Validated: acceptable but requires additional supervised mission.
- Not Validated: insufficient mission evidence or substandard outcome.

Graduation credentialing is provisional until real mission validation is complete.

## 11. AEIS Integration

AEIS (Atlas Executive Intelligence System) serves as the governance and intelligence integration layer for Atlas Academy learning operations.

AEIS integration responsibilities:
- Align capability objectives to enterprise mission priorities.
- Track performance telemetry across training and missions.
- Maintain decision-grade records for evaluation and certification.
- Detect competency drift and trigger recertification workflows.
- Provide executive visibility into capability readiness and risk.

AEIS-aligned educational controls:
- Capability records must be machine-readable and auditable.
- School dashboards must report objective attainment and maturity distribution.
- Critical skill gaps must generate remediation alerts.
- Curriculum revisions must include traceability to AEIS performance signals.

## 12. Continuous Improvement Process

Atlas Academy schools must run a formal continuous improvement cycle.

Improvement cycle cadence:
1. Collect: gather assessment results, mission outcomes, and reviewer feedback.
2. Diagnose: identify systemic weaknesses, bottlenecks, and error patterns.
3. Prioritize: rank improvement opportunities by mission impact and risk reduction.
4. Update: revise objectives, methods, rubrics, and learning assets.
5. Validate: test updates through pilot cohorts or controlled implementation.
6. Institutionalize: adopt validated updates as new standard practice.

Improvement governance rules:
- Major standard changes require review board approval.
- Version all curriculum changes with rationale and expected impact.
- Maintain backward traceability from outcomes to curriculum revisions.

## 13. Professional Standards

All Atlas Academy schools and graduates must uphold common professional standards.

Required standards:
- Accuracy: avoid material factual error.
- Integrity: represent evidence and uncertainty honestly.
- Accountability: maintain transparent decision and evidence trails.
- Confidentiality: protect sensitive information according to policy.
- Fairness: avoid biased reasoning and unjustified exclusion.
- Reliability: produce consistent quality over time.
- Professional conduct: maintain disciplined, respectful, mission-aligned behavior.

Critical violations include fabrication, evidence suppression, misrepresentation of certainty, and deliberate standard circumvention.

Critical violations trigger immediate review and may invalidate certification.

All future Atlas Academy schools must author curricula using the template below.

Required curriculum template:
1. School Purpose and Domain Scope
2. Professional Competency Definition
3. Capability Targets and Maturity Threshold
4. Learning Objectives
5. Required Mental Models
6. Skill Decomposition Map
7. Practice Mission Progression
8. Objective Evaluation Rubrics
9. Required Deliverables and Evidence Artifacts
10. Graduation Criteria and Capstone Design
11. Real Mission Validation Plan
12. AEIS Measurement and Reporting Integration
13. Continuous Improvement and Versioning Process
14. Professional Standards and Ethics Requirements

Template compliance rules:
- Every section is mandatory.
- Each objective must map to measurable evaluation criteria.
- Critical objectives must define failure consequences and remediation paths.
- Curriculum release is blocked until governance review approves completeness.
