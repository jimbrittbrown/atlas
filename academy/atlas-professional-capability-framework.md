# ATLAS PROFESSIONAL CAPABILITY FRAMEWORK (APCF)
## Version 1.0

## Purpose

The Atlas Professional Capability Framework (APCF) defines the universal architecture for designing, operating, validating, and governing every professional capability in Atlas.

This framework establishes a common, layered model so all capabilities are:
- Architecturally consistent.
- Professionally measurable.
- Governable at executive level.
- Operationally deployable in production environments.
- Continuously improvable through intelligence feedback.

This is a governance document. It defines structure, authority, standards, and interaction patterns. It does not define implementation code.

## Layer 1: Academy Layer

### Purpose
Define capability education standards, learning pathways, and certification requirements so each capability is developed through a disciplined professional curriculum.

### Responsibilities
- Define curriculum structure, objectives, and progression.
- Establish capability maturity expectations.
- Specify practice mission pathways and evidence artifacts.
- Set graduation thresholds for professional readiness.
- Maintain curriculum versioning and educational governance records.

### Inputs
- Enterprise capability priorities.
- Historical performance data from deployed capabilities.
- Professional standards and regulatory constraints.
- Lessons learned from mission outcomes.

### Outputs
- Capability curriculum blueprint.
- Objective mastery rubrics.
- Learning and assessment artifacts.
- Graduation and recertification requirements.

### Decision Authority
- Approves educational architecture for capabilities.
- Approves curriculum versions and educational standards.
- Recommends readiness status to Director Layer.

### Success Criteria
- Curriculum completeness against APCF standards.
- Measurable objective coverage for all critical skills.
- High alignment between training outcomes and mission requirements.
- Low remediation burden after deployment.

### Interaction With Other Layers
- Receives strategic priorities from Director Layer.
- Provides readiness outputs to Execution Engine Layer.
- Receives quality insights from Quality Intelligence Layer.
- Aligns educational telemetry requirements with AEIS Layer.

## Layer 2: Director Layer

### Purpose
Provide strategic direction, operating scope, resource prioritization, and governance decisions for each professional capability lifecycle.

### Responsibilities
- Define mission-aligned capability strategy.
- Set capability scope boundaries and non-negotiable constraints.
- Prioritize development and deployment sequence.
- Resolve cross-layer conflicts and tradeoffs.
- Authorize progression between lifecycle stages.

### Inputs
- Academy readiness recommendations.
- Executive priorities and risk appetite.
- Quality performance summaries.
- AEIS intelligence on enterprise capability posture.

### Outputs
- Strategic capability charter.
- Stage-gate decisions and priorities.
- Risk directives and escalation decisions.
- Resource allocation mandates.

### Decision Authority
- Final authority on capability scope and priority.
- Stage-gate approval authority for capability advancement.
- Conflict arbitration authority across operational layers.

### Success Criteria
- Capabilities remain aligned to enterprise mission outcomes.
- Stage-gate decisions are timely and evidence-based.
- Resource allocation matches strategic value and risk.
- Reduced strategic drift across capability portfolio.

### Interaction With Other Layers
- Directs Academy Layer targets and constraints.
- Authorizes Execution Engine activation and scaling.
- Requests assurance from Quality Intelligence Layer.
- Escalates high-stakes decisions to Executive Council Layer.
- Uses AEIS Layer intelligence for prioritization.

## Layer 3: Execution Engine Layer

### Purpose
Operationalize capabilities into repeatable workflows, mission execution patterns, and production-relevant outputs.

### Responsibilities
- Translate capability designs into executable operating procedures.
- Run practice and live mission workflows.
- Capture execution artifacts, decision traces, and performance metrics.
- Enforce operational controls and fallback protocols.
- Maintain execution consistency under real constraints.

### Inputs
- Certified capability standards from Academy Layer.
- Strategic directives from Director Layer.
- Operating constraints and risk controls.
- Mission requirements from production stakeholders.

### Outputs
- Mission deliverables.
- Execution logs and evidence trails.
- Performance and exception reports.
- Operational lessons for curriculum and quality refinement.

### Decision Authority
- Tactical authority over real-time execution decisions within approved boundaries.
- Authority to pause, reroute, or escalate workflows on control breaches.
- No authority to alter strategic or governance standards.

### Success Criteria
- Consistent output quality under defined constraints.
- High traceability of decisions and artifacts.
- Low rate of critical execution failures.
- Effective handling of exceptions and edge cases.

### Interaction With Other Layers
- Executes standards defined by Academy Layer.
- Operates under Director Layer constraints.
- Feeds performance data to Quality Intelligence Layer.
- Provides mission telemetry to AEIS Layer.
- Delivers production outputs through Production Integration Layer.

## Layer 4: Quality Intelligence Layer

### Purpose
Provide independent evaluation, performance intelligence, risk detection, and corrective guidance for professional capability quality.

### Responsibilities
- Evaluate mission outputs against objective standards.
- Detect error patterns, drift, and systemic quality risks.
- Validate reliability, traceability, and professional compliance.
- Recommend remediation and standard improvements.
- Produce quality confidence ratings for decision-makers.

### Inputs
- Execution artifacts and mission outputs.
- Evaluation rubrics and competency criteria.
- Incident and exception records.
- AEIS trend data and comparative benchmarks.

### Outputs
- Quality assurance reports.
- Capability confidence scores.
- Root-cause and drift analyses.
- Corrective action recommendations.

### Decision Authority
- Authority to issue quality findings and risk flags.
- Authority to recommend stage rollback or remediation.
- Advisory authority to Director and Executive Council for major decisions.

### Success Criteria
- Early detection of quality degradation.
- High precision and usefulness of quality assessments.
- Reduced recurrence of known failure modes.
- Demonstrable improvement in capability reliability over time.

### Interaction With Other Layers
- Audits Execution Engine performance.
- Informs Academy Layer curriculum updates.
- Advises Director Layer on gate progression risks.
- Provides risk evidence to Executive Council Layer.
- Shares quality telemetry with AEIS Layer.

## Layer 5: Executive Council Layer

### Purpose
Provide top-level governance, policy enforcement, escalation adjudication, and final accountability for enterprise-critical capability decisions.

### Responsibilities
- Approve or reject high-impact capability decisions.
- Set governance policy, risk tolerance, and compliance expectations.
- Adjudicate escalated conflicts across layers.
- Authorize exceptional deviations from standard frameworks.
- Ensure capability portfolio integrity at enterprise level.

### Inputs
- Escalation packages from Director Layer.
- Quality risk assessments from Quality Intelligence Layer.
- Strategic posture from AEIS Layer.
- Production impact assessments.

### Outputs
- Executive decisions and policy directives.
- Escalation resolutions.
- Governance exceptions or enforcement mandates.
- Enterprise-level capability risk posture statements.

### Decision Authority
- Highest authority for governance and escalation decisions.
- Authority to halt, re-scope, or terminate capabilities.
- Authority to mandate remediation before continuation.

### Success Criteria
- Governance consistency across all capabilities.
- Timely, defensible decisions on high-stakes issues.
- Controlled risk exposure in production environments.
- High trust in executive oversight outcomes.

### Interaction With Other Layers
- Receives escalations from Director Layer.
- Uses Quality Intelligence findings for governance judgments.
- Aligns policy signals to Academy and Execution layers.
- Integrates AEIS intelligence for enterprise decisions.
- Authorizes production-impacting directives in Production Integration Layer.

## Layer 6: AEIS Layer

### Purpose
Serve as the enterprise intelligence, measurement, and governance signal layer that unifies capability data for decision-grade oversight.

### Responsibilities
- Aggregate cross-layer telemetry and outcomes.
- Maintain auditable records of capability lifecycle states.
- Generate readiness, risk, and performance intelligence.
- Trigger alerts for drift, degradation, or control breaches.
- Provide dashboards and evidence for governance bodies.

### Inputs
- Educational outcomes from Academy Layer.
- Strategic and gate decisions from Director Layer.
- Mission telemetry from Execution Engine Layer.
- Quality findings from Quality Intelligence Layer.
- Governance directives from Executive Council Layer.
- Production performance signals from Production Integration Layer.

### Outputs
- Unified capability intelligence dashboards.
- Readiness and risk alerts.
- Governance reporting packages.
- Trend analytics for continuous improvement.

### Decision Authority
- No unilateral strategic authority.
- Authority to trigger required review events based on thresholds.
- Authority to enforce data completeness and auditability standards.

### Success Criteria
- High data integrity and coverage across all layers.
- Reliable early warning signals for risk and drift.
- Strong decision support value for governance forums.
- Full audit traceability of capability lifecycle history.

### Interaction With Other Layers
- Ingests and normalizes data from all layers.
- Provides intelligence feedback to every layer.
- Enables evidence-based decisions by Director and Executive Council layers.
- Supports Academy and Quality layers in standards refinement.

## Layer 7: Production Integration Layer

### Purpose
Integrate validated professional capabilities into live operating environments with controlled rollout, monitoring, and operational safeguards.

### Responsibilities
- Translate validated capability outputs into production workflows.
- Enforce deployment gates, controls, and rollback criteria.
- Monitor real-world impact and operational stability.
- Coordinate incident response and recovery pathways.
- Feed production outcomes back into governance and learning loops.

### Inputs
- Deployment authorization from Director and Executive Council layers.
- Validated capability outputs from Execution Engine Layer.
- Quality confidence ratings and controls.
- AEIS readiness and risk signals.

### Outputs
- Production deployment decisions and records.
- Operational performance metrics.
- Incident and recovery reports.
- Production-informed improvement recommendations.

### Decision Authority
- Authority to approve, defer, or rollback deployment within policy.
- Authority to activate incident containment procedures.
- No authority to bypass executive governance controls.

### Success Criteria
- Safe and stable capability operation in production.
- Minimal high-severity incidents.
- Fast detection and mitigation of operational failures.
- Strong feedback quality into improvement cycles.

### Interaction With Other Layers
- Receives validated capabilities from Execution Engine Layer.
- Applies quality controls from Quality Intelligence Layer.
- Operates under governance from Director and Executive Council layers.
- Reports live telemetry and incidents to AEIS Layer.
- Returns real mission data to Academy Layer for curriculum refinement.

## Cross-Layer Governance Rules

1. No layer may claim authority outside its defined decision boundaries.
2. Every critical decision must be supported by auditable evidence.
3. Quality findings and AEIS risk alerts trigger mandatory review workflows.
4. Production deployment requires explicit validation and governance authorization.
5. Curriculum, execution, quality, and production loops must remain continuously connected.
6. Escalation precedence: Execution Engine -> Director -> Executive Council.
7. Framework deviations require documented exception approval.

## Standard Capability Flow (Reference Pattern)

1. Academy Layer defines and certifies capability standard.
2. Director Layer authorizes scope, priority, and stage progression.
3. Execution Engine Layer operationalizes and runs capability missions.
4. Quality Intelligence Layer validates outcomes and flags risk.
5. AEIS Layer consolidates telemetry and issues intelligence signals.
6. Executive Council Layer resolves escalations and sets governance direction.
7. Production Integration Layer deploys validated capability into live operations.
8. Continuous feedback loops update all upstream layers.

## Adoption Scope

This APCF document is the architectural template for every current and future professional capability inside Atlas.

All future capability documents must explicitly map their design to these seven layers and demonstrate compliance with this governance structure.
