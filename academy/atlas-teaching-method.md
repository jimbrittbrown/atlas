# ATLAS ACADEMY
## Atlas Teaching Method (ATM)
### Version 1.0

## Purpose

The Atlas Teaching Method (ATM) defines the universal methodology for how Atlas learns professional capabilities across all Atlas Academy schools.

This standard governs learning mechanics, evaluation loops, feedback integration, and readiness validation. It does not define domain content. Domain curricula define what Atlas learns; ATM defines how Atlas learns.

This is a governance document and applies to every current and future school in Atlas Academy.

## 1. Educational Cycle

### Definition
The educational cycle is the core learning loop used to build capability from initial exposure to professional readiness.

### ATM Educational Cycle
1. Orient: establish mission context, performance objectives, and constraints.
2. Observe: study professional exemplars and decision patterns.
3. Practice: execute structured tasks with increasing realism.
4. Critique: evaluate performance through self and external review.
5. Reflect: convert findings into updated mental models and operating rules.
6. Validate: test capability in mission-representative conditions.
7. Certify: approve progression when objective thresholds are met.
8. Refine: update methods based on outcome intelligence.

### Cycle Rule
No learner advances by content completion alone. Advancement requires evidence of capability improvement at each cycle stage.

## 2. Professional Observation

### Purpose
Professional observation develops pattern recognition, judgment calibration, and standards awareness before independent execution.

### Observation Requirements
Each program must include:
- Expert workflow observation.
- Decision rationale analysis.
- Error and recovery pattern analysis.
- Comparison of strong and weak professional outputs.

### Observation Artifacts
Required artifacts include:
- Observation log.
- Decision pattern notes.
- Quality indicator map.
- Initial professional benchmark profile.

### Observation Standard
Observation must produce explicit, transferable lessons that inform practice design.

## 3. Practice Methodology

### Purpose
Practice converts conceptual understanding into reliable operational behavior.

### Practice Structure
Practice must progress through four stages:
1. Guided practice: high structure, low ambiguity.
2. Structured practice: bounded complexity and partial independence.
3. Independent practice: realistic tasks with minimal scaffolding.
4. Stress practice: adversarial conditions, uncertainty, and conflicting signals.

### Practice Design Rules
- Every practice mission maps to specific capability objectives.
- Every mission has predefined success criteria.
- Every mission generates evidence artifacts for evaluation.
- Difficulty progression is staged and explicit.

### Practice Completion Rule
Practice is complete only when output quality, reasoning quality, and process traceability all meet standard.

## 4. Self-Critique Methodology

### Purpose
Self-critique develops professional judgment, error detection, and self-correction capability.

### Self-Critique Protocol
After each mission, the learner must evaluate:
- Objective attainment.
- Quality gaps.
- Reasoning errors.
- Assumption failures.
- Process breakdowns.
- Risk handling performance.

### Required Self-Critique Outputs
- Structured self-assessment report.
- Error taxonomy entry.
- Corrective action plan.
- Confidence rating with justification.

### Self-Critique Integrity Rule
Self-critique must include both successes and failures; omission of material weaknesses is non-compliant.

## 5. External Critique Methodology

### Purpose
External critique provides independent quality assurance and prevents blind spots in self-assessment.

### External Critique Requirements
- Reviewer independence from mission execution.
- Rubric-based evaluation against objective standards.
- Explicit finding classification (critical, major, minor, advisory).
- Actionable remediation guidance.

### Required External Critique Outputs
- Evaluation scorecard.
- Findings report with evidence references.
- Risk implications summary.
- Remediation directives and re-evaluation conditions.

### External Critique Authority Rule
Critical external findings override self-assessed readiness until remediated and revalidated.

## 6. Reflection Process

### Purpose
Reflection transforms critique and mission evidence into durable professional learning.

### Reflection Process Steps
1. Consolidate evidence from observation, practice, and critiques.
2. Identify recurring strengths and failure patterns.
3. Update mental models and operational heuristics.
4. Define next-cycle improvements.
5. Record validated lessons for future missions.

### Reflection Artifacts
- Reflection journal entry.
- Updated mental model map.
- Improvement priorities for next cycle.
- Risk controls to prevent repeated failures.

### Reflection Rule
Reflection is mandatory at the end of each educational cycle and must produce operationally specific updates.

## 7. AEIS Integration

### Purpose
AEIS integration ensures teaching outcomes are measured, governed, and aligned with enterprise capability priorities.

### AEIS Integration Requirements
- Track objective attainment and progression velocity.
- Monitor quality trends and drift indicators.
- Record critique findings and remediation completion.
- Generate readiness signals for stage-gate decisions.
- Maintain auditable learner evidence records.

### AEIS Outputs For ATM
- Learning performance dashboard.
- Risk and drift alerts.
- Readiness status summaries.
- Continuous improvement intelligence reports.

### AEIS Control Rule
No graduation decision is valid without AEIS-complete evidence records for required objectives.

## 8. Mission Validation

### Purpose
Mission validation confirms that learned capability is usable under real operational constraints.

### Validation Requirements
- At least one production-representative mission.
- Performance under realistic uncertainty and constraints.
- Independent review of outputs and reasoning trace.
- Confirmation of professional standards compliance.

### Validation Outcomes
- Validated.
- Provisionally validated (additional mission required).
- Not validated.

### Validation Rule
Classroom or simulation success alone cannot certify professional readiness.

## 9. Continuous Refinement

### Purpose
Continuous refinement ensures ATM remains effective as missions, risks, and professional standards evolve.

### Refinement Cycle
1. Collect mission and evaluation data.
2. Diagnose method weaknesses and bottlenecks.
3. Prioritize improvements by impact and risk reduction.
4. Update teaching practices, rubrics, and stage criteria.
5. Pilot updates in controlled cohorts.
6. Adopt validated updates into standard ATM policy.

### Refinement Governance Rules
- Significant methodology changes require governance approval.
- All updates must be versioned with rationale.
- Backward traceability between outcomes and changes is required.

## 10. Graduation Methodology

### Purpose
Graduation methodology defines how final professional readiness is determined under ATM.

### Graduation Gates
A learner graduates only when all gates are passed:
1. Objective mastery gate: critical objectives at required level.
2. Quality gate: outputs meet professional standards.
3. Reliability gate: consistent performance across repeated missions.
4. Critique gate: critical findings fully remediated.
5. Mission validation gate: real mission performance validated.
6. Evidence gate: complete auditable record in AEIS.

### Graduation Decisions
- Pass.
- Conditional pass (targeted remediation required; no critical failures).
- Fail.

### Graduation Authority
Final graduation decisions require formal review against ATM gates and evidence package.

## Adoption And Compliance

ATM is the mandatory teaching methodology for every Atlas Academy school.

Compliance requirements:
1. Every school must map its curriculum delivery model to ATM.
2. Every school must implement ATM artifacts and evaluation controls.
3. Any deviation from ATM requires formal governance exception approval.

Non-compliant programs are not eligible for Atlas Academy certification authority.
