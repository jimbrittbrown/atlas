# ATLAS ACADEMY
## Atlas Director Certification Framework (ADCF)
### Version 1.0

## Constitutional Purpose

The Atlas Director Certification Framework (ADCF) defines how Atlas Academy graduates professional capabilities into operational Director Profiles used by the Atlas production runtime.

ADCF is the formal bridge between education and execution. It governs certification, authorization, lifecycle management, and performance feedback for professional Director capability in production environments.

This is a governance architecture document. It defines standards and authority, not implementation.

## 1. Purpose Of Director Certification

Director Certification exists to ensure only professionally validated capabilities are authorized to operate in production decision and execution pathways.

Director Certification purposes:
- Convert educational achievement into operational trust.
- Protect production systems from uncertified or degraded capability.
- Standardize readiness thresholds across professional domains.
- Enable accountable deployment decisions with auditable evidence.
- Provide governance continuity from Academy graduation to runtime authority.

No Director capability may enter production without certification under ADCF.

## 2. Relationship Between Professional Schools And Directors

Professional Schools develop and evaluate capabilities.
Director Certification authorizes those capabilities for operational runtime use.

Relationship rules:
- Schools produce competency evidence aligned with ACF.
- ADCF validates that evidence against production authorization criteria.
- Only school-certified capabilities are eligible for Director Profile registration.
- School graduation is necessary but not sufficient for production authority.

Schools create professional readiness; ADCF confers operational legitimacy.

## 3. Director Professional Profiles

A Director Professional Profile is the production identity package representing a certified professional capability.

Required profile components:
- Director identity and professional domain.
- Certified capability list and scope boundaries.
- Authorized decision domains.
- Escalation boundaries and prohibited actions.
- Minimum competency level and evidence references.
- Version status and certification validity window.
- Performance risk flags and special controls.

Profile governance rule:
- Director Profiles must be explicit, bounded, and traceable to certification artifacts.

## 4. Certified Capability Registry

ADCF requires a Certified Capability Registry as the authoritative record of all production-eligible capabilities.

Registry minimum fields:
- Capability identifier.
- Origin school and program.
- Certification status (active, conditional, suspended, expired, retired).
- Certified competency level.
- Capability version.
- Effective and expiration dates.
- Re-certification due date.
- Linked Director Profile(s).
- Governing exception records (if any).

Registry rules:
- Registry is the source of truth for production authorization checks.
- Unregistered capabilities are treated as unauthorized.

## 5. Competency Levels

ADCF adopts ACF competency progression logic for certification decisions.

Production certification interpretation:
- Level 1-2: educational progression only; not production-authorizable.
- Level 3: minimum production eligibility for bounded operational scope.
- Level 4: eligible for high-complexity operational scope.
- Level 5: eligible for strategic and supervisory director functions.

Competency governance rules:
- Scope of authorization must match certified level.
- Higher-risk domains may require level thresholds above baseline policy.

## 6. Production Authorization

Production Authorization is the governance decision permitting a certified Director Profile to execute within runtime boundaries.

Authorization gates:
1. Valid school and capability certification evidence.
2. ACF-aligned competency threshold met.
3. No unresolved critical external evaluation findings.
4. AEIS record completeness and integrity check passed.
5. Runtime scope boundaries documented and approved.
6. Executive governance approval where required by risk policy.

Authorization outcomes:
- Authorized.
- Conditionally Authorized (limited scope, additional controls).
- Not Authorized.

## 7. Capability Versioning

All certified capabilities must be version-governed.

Versioning requirements:
- Semantic version identifier for capability definition.
- Change classification (minor tuning, major standard shift, critical risk patch).
- Traceable rationale and expected production impact.
- Compatibility assessment with existing Director Profiles.
- Re-certification impact statement.

Versioning rules:
- Major or critical changes trigger certification review.
- Production authorization may be limited pending version compliance review.

## 8. Continuing Professional Education

Certified Directors must continue professional development to maintain relevance and reliability.

Continuing education requirements:
- Periodic update modules for standards, risk controls, and domain shifts.
- Documented completion of governance-mandated update training.
- Evidence of applied learning in mission-relevant scenarios.
- Targeted education in response to quality or incident findings.

Policy rule:
- Continuing education non-compliance may trigger conditional status or suspension.

## 9. Re-Certification Requirements

Certification validity is time-bound and performance-bound.

Re-certification triggers:
- Scheduled renewal interval.
- Material capability version changes.
- AEIS-detected performance regression.
- Major production incident linked to capability behavior.

Re-certification evidence requirements:
- Updated competency evaluation package.
- Recent production performance review.
- External professional evaluation outcome.
- Completion of required continuing education.

Re-certification outcomes:
- Renewed.
- Renewed with conditions.
- Denied.

## 10. Capability Suspension

Suspension is a protective governance action removing or restricting production authority.

Suspension triggers:
- Critical integrity violation.
- Repeated severe performance failure.
- Non-compliance with re-certification obligations.
- Material risk escalation detected by AEIS or governance review.

Suspension modes:
- Full suspension (no production authority).
- Partial suspension (scope-limited authority).
- Conditional suspension (temporary restrictions pending remediation).

Suspension release requirements:
- Root-cause remediation completion.
- Verified corrective evidence.
- Formal reinstatement approval.

## 11. AEIS Performance Feedback

AEIS provides continuous intelligence for Director certification health.

Required AEIS feedback signals:
- Capability performance trend analysis.
- Drift and degradation alerts.
- Incident association and recurrence patterns.
- Quality confidence ratings.
- Re-certification risk forecasting.
- Scope adequacy versus performance outcomes.

AEIS governance rule:
- Significant risk signals trigger mandatory certification review workflows.

## 12. Executive Council Recommendations

Executive Council provides strategic governance recommendations on certification policy, risk posture, and authorization scope.

Recommendation domains:
- Raise or lower authorization thresholds by risk class.
- Approve or deny high-stakes capability deployment.
- Mandate remediation programs for systemic issues.
- Require policy updates when enterprise risk conditions change.

Authority rule:
- Executive Council recommendations are binding when ratified under governance charter.

## 13. Production Runtime Integration

ADCF governs how certified Director Profiles are integrated into Atlas production runtime pathways.

Integration requirements:
- Runtime access checks against Certified Capability Registry.
- Scope enforcement based on Director Profile authority boundaries.
- Real-time escalation routing for out-of-scope decisions.
- Logging and traceability of director-invoked actions.
- Feedback loop from runtime outcomes to AEIS and certification governance.

Integration rule:
- Runtime must treat certification status as a hard control, not an advisory signal.

## 14. Relationship To AEP

AEP defines constitutional educational philosophy.
ADCF operationalizes how professionally learned capability becomes production-authorized director authority.

Relationship rules:
- ADCF must preserve AEP principles: competency over knowledge, evidence over assertion, integrity over speed.
- If ADCF process conflicts with AEP constitutional philosophy, AEP prevails.

## 15. Relationship To ATM

ATM defines how learning is conducted.
ADCF defines how learned outcomes are certified for production director operation.

Relationship rules:
- ATM artifacts must provide evidence usable by ADCF authorization gates.
- ATM completion alone does not confer Director authority.

## 16. Relationship To ACF

ACF defines competency measurement and graduation architecture.
ADCF uses ACF results as primary certification evidence.

Relationship rules:
- ADCF competency decisions must map to ACF levels and dimensions.
- Re-certification and regression handling must remain ACF-consistent.

## 17. Relationship To APSB

APSB defines the architecture for all professional schools.
ADCF consumes APSB-conformant school outputs for production certification decisions.

Relationship rules:
- Only APSB-compliant schools may submit capabilities for Director certification.
- School-level deviations affecting certification validity require governance review.

## 18. Director Lifecycle

ADCF defines a universal Director lifecycle.

Lifecycle stages:
1. Candidate Formation: capability developed in professional school.
2. Educational Certification: capability passes school and ACF gates.
3. Director Profile Creation: professional scope and authority boundaries documented.
4. Production Authorization: governance decision grants runtime authority.
5. Active Operation: director executes within approved scope.
6. Continuous Monitoring: AEIS tracks performance, drift, and risk.
7. Continuing Education: required updates and applied learning.
8. Re-Certification: periodic or trigger-based certification renewal.
9. Restriction Or Suspension: authority reduced or removed when risk requires.
10. Reinstatement Or Retirement: capability restored after remediation or retired permanently.

Lifecycle rule:
- Director authority is dynamic and contingent on continuing evidence of competency and integrity.

## Governance Applicability

ADCF applies to:
- Atlas Academy governance and certification bodies.
- All professional schools submitting capabilities for production use.
- All Director Profiles and runtime authorization decisions.
- All certification, recertification, suspension, and reinstatement workflows.

ADCF is the constitutional bridge standard from educational graduation to production execution authority.
