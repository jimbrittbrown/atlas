# ATLAS ACADEMY
## Atlas Competency Framework (ACF)
### Version 1.0

## Constitutional Purpose

The Atlas Competency Framework (ACF) is the constitutional governance standard for evaluating, measuring, certifying, and renewing professional competency across all Atlas Academy capabilities.

ACF defines how competency is measured and graduated. It does not define curriculum content and does not define implementation mechanics.

ACF is inherited by every current and future Atlas Academy school as mandatory competency architecture.

## 1. Purpose Of Competency Measurement

Competency measurement exists to ensure Atlas Academy credentials represent proven professional capability, not theoretical familiarity.

The purposes of competency measurement are to:
- Protect mission quality and reduce operational risk.
- Ensure consistency and comparability across schools and professions.
- Validate readiness for production-representative and live mission environments.
- Enable defensible graduation and recertification decisions.
- Support institutional learning through measurable performance data.

Competency measurement is a governance function, not a ceremonial checkpoint.

## 2. Competency Vs Knowledge

### Knowledge
Knowledge is possession of facts, concepts, models, and procedural awareness.

### Competency
Competency is demonstrated ability to apply knowledge with sound judgment and reliable execution under realistic constraints.

### Constitutional Distinction
- Knowledge can be declared by explanation.
- Competency must be demonstrated through evidence.
- Certification requires competency, not knowledge alone.

## 3. Competency Progression Levels

ACF defines a universal five-level progression model.

Level 1: Foundational Awareness
- Recognizes core concepts and terminology.
- Cannot perform independently.

Level 2: Guided Application
- Performs bounded tasks with supervision.
- Limited handling of ambiguity and exceptions.

Level 3: Professional Competence
- Performs required tasks reliably in routine and moderately complex conditions.
- Demonstrates traceable reasoning and acceptable risk control.

Level 4: Advanced Professional Performance
- Operates effectively in high complexity and conflicting-signal conditions.
- Anticipates failure modes and manages uncertainty proactively.

Level 5: Strategic Professional Mastery
- Sets quality standards, improves systems, and mentors others.
- Sustains high-integrity performance in high-stakes environments.

Default graduation threshold for schools: Level 3 minimum unless governance explicitly raises the threshold.

## 4. Knowledge Evaluation

### Purpose
Assess whether the learner possesses the conceptual and factual base required for competency.

### Evaluation Criteria
- Accuracy of core facts and concepts.
- Structural understanding of frameworks and models.
- Contextual relevance of recalled knowledge.
- Correct identification of limits and assumptions.

### Evidence Forms
- Structured written responses.
- Concept maps and framework mappings.
- Case-based knowledge checks.

### Rule
Knowledge evaluation is required but cannot independently authorize graduation.

## 5. Understanding Evaluation

### Purpose
Assess whether the learner can explain meaning, relationships, causality, and implications.

### Evaluation Criteria
- Correct explanation of why methods or decisions apply.
- Ability to distinguish principle from procedure.
- Ability to transfer understanding to novel but related scenarios.
- Explicit articulation of boundary conditions.

### Evidence Forms
- Scenario interpretation analyses.
- Comparative reasoning exercises.
- Assumption and limitation statements.

### Rule
Understanding is valid only when explanations remain coherent under challenge.

## 6. Decision-Making Evaluation

### Purpose
Measure quality of professional judgment under uncertainty, risk, and competing priorities.

### Evaluation Criteria
- Problem framing quality.
- Option generation and tradeoff analysis.
- Risk identification and mitigation logic.
- Ethical and governance compliance in decisions.
- Escalation quality when authority boundaries are reached.

### Evidence Forms
- Decision logs with rationale traces.
- Tradeoff matrices.
- Escalation records and outcomes.

### Rule
Unjustified or non-traceable decisions are considered non-competent regardless of output quality.

## 7. Execution Evaluation

### Purpose
Evaluate whether the learner can reliably produce professional-grade outputs.

### Evaluation Criteria
- Output correctness and completeness.
- Process discipline and control adherence.
- Reliability across repeated tasks.
- Exception handling and recovery behavior.
- Timeliness under realistic constraints.

### Evidence Forms
- Mission deliverables.
- Workflow and process logs.
- Quality control records.
- Repeat-trial performance summaries.

### Rule
Single-instance success is insufficient; competency requires repeatable performance.

## 8. Self-Evaluation Methodology

### Purpose
Develop professional self-monitoring, calibration, and corrective behavior.

### Required Self-Evaluation Components
- Objective attainment review.
- Error and assumption analysis.
- Confidence rating with rationale.
- Corrective action plan with measurable follow-up.

### Method Controls
- Structured self-evaluation template required.
- Claims must reference objective evidence.
- Material omissions invalidate self-assessment quality.

### Rule
Self-evaluation informs progression but cannot independently certify competency.

## 9. External Professional Evaluation

### Purpose
Provide independent, standards-based judgment to reduce bias and blind spots.

### Required External Evaluation Controls
- Reviewer independence from learner execution.
- Rubric-based assessment against declared standards.
- Classification of findings: critical, major, minor, advisory.
- Explicit remediation directives for substandard performance.

### Evidence Forms
- External scorecards.
- Finding reports with supporting evidence.
- Re-evaluation outcomes after remediation.

### Rule
Critical external findings block graduation until remediated and revalidated.

## 10. Production Performance Evaluation

### Purpose
Confirm competency validity under production-representative or live mission conditions.

### Evaluation Criteria
- Performance quality under realistic uncertainty.
- Operational reliability and control compliance.
- Stakeholder utility of outputs.
- Incident response and recovery quality.

### Evidence Forms
- Production or production-representative mission records.
- Outcome quality reviews.
- Incident and corrective action logs.

### Rule
Simulation-only performance cannot establish full professional competency certification.

## 11. Evidence-Based Graduation

Graduation is a formal governance judgment based on complete evidence package review.

### Mandatory Graduation Evidence Gates
1. Knowledge and understanding thresholds met.
2. Decision-making quality meets standard.
3. Execution reliability demonstrated across repeated missions.
4. External evaluation contains no unresolved critical findings.
5. Production performance evaluation status is validated or governance-approved equivalent.
6. Evidence traceability and audit completeness confirmed.

### Graduation Outcomes
- Pass.
- Conditional Pass (targeted remediation; no critical unresolved failures).
- Fail.

### Rule
No learner graduates based on completion status, self-confidence, or informal recommendation.

## 12. Competency Regression And Re-Certification

### Regression Principle
Competency can degrade over time due to drift, environmental change, or prolonged non-use.

### Regression Triggers
- Decline in quality or reliability metrics.
- Repeated critical findings.
- Material changes in professional standards.
- Significant production incidents linked to capability gaps.

### Re-Certification Requirements
- Periodic competency reassessment at governance-defined intervals.
- Trigger-based reassessment when regression signals exceed thresholds.
- Targeted remediation for regressed domains.
- Formal recertification decision based on updated evidence.

### Rule
Certification status is maintainable only through ongoing demonstrated competency.

## 13. Relationship To AEP

AEP defines why Atlas learns and what professional learning means.
ACF defines how professional competency is measured and certified.

Relationship rule:
- ACF must operationalize AEP principles, especially competency-over-knowledge and evidence-based legitimacy.
- If ACF procedure conflicts with AEP constitutional philosophy, AEP prevails.

## 14. Relationship To ATM

ATM defines how learning is conducted.
ACF defines how competency outcomes from learning are evaluated and graduated.

Relationship rule:
- ATM learning artifacts must map to ACF evidence requirements.
- ATM progression and ACF certification gates must remain aligned.
- Method success in ATM does not imply certification unless ACF gates are passed.

## 15. Relationship To AEIS

AEIS is the intelligence and governance instrumentation layer for ACF operations.

AEIS responsibilities in ACF context:
- Maintain auditable competency evidence records.
- Track progression, drift, and recertification status.
- Generate readiness and risk signals for certification decisions.
- Support portfolio-level comparability across schools and capabilities.

Rule:
- Certification and recertification decisions require AEIS-complete records.

## 16. Relationship To All Professional Schools

ACF is mandatory constitutional competency architecture for every Atlas Academy school.

School inheritance requirements:
- Every school must map assessments to ACF dimensions.
- Every school must define progression and graduation thresholds in ACF terms.
- Every school must implement regression detection and recertification pathways.
- Any school-level deviation requires formal governance exception approval.

No school is competency-governance compliant without explicit ACF alignment.

## Constitutional Competency Principles

1. Competency is demonstrated capability, not conceptual familiarity.
2. Measurement must be objective, traceable, and auditable.
3. Certification requires multi-dimensional evidence, not single metric success.
4. External evaluation is mandatory for professional legitimacy.
5. Production-representative validation is required for real readiness.
6. Competency is dynamic and must be recertified over time.
7. ACF is subordinate to AEP philosophy and coordinated with ATM method.
8. AEIS is required for competency governance integrity.

## Governance Applicability

ACF applies to:
- All Atlas Academy schools and professional tracks.
- All competency assessments and graduation decisions.
- All certification and recertification governance workflows.

ACF has constitutional authority over competency architecture across the Atlas Academy educational stack.
