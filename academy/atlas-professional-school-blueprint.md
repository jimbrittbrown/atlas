# ATLAS ACADEMY
## Atlas Professional School Blueprint (APSB)
### Version 1.0

## Constitutional Purpose

The Atlas Professional School Blueprint (APSB) is the universal governance template for designing, operating, and evolving every professional school within Atlas Academy.

APSB is reusable by design. It does not define a specific school domain. It defines the required architecture, governance controls, lifecycle stages, and institutional interfaces that every school must inherit.

APSB is a governance document only. It defines structure and standards, not implementation.

## 1. School Purpose

### Requirement
Each professional school must define a clear purpose statement that explains why the school exists and what institutional value it creates.

### Required Purpose Elements
- Domain scope and boundaries.
- Strategic contribution to Atlas mission outcomes.
- Primary professional outcomes expected from graduates.
- Risk if this school does not exist or underperforms.

### Governance Rule
A school is not eligible for activation without an approved purpose statement.

## 2. Professional Mission

### Requirement
Each school must declare the mission profile of the profession it develops.

### Required Mission Elements
- Who the profession serves.
- What mission outcomes it must reliably produce.
- Operating constraints and non-negotiable limitations.
- Conditions under which escalation is required.

### Governance Rule
Mission statements must be measurable, bounded, and auditable.

## 3. Professional Identity

### Requirement
Each school must define the identity model of its target profession before curriculum development begins.

### Required Identity Elements
- Professional title and role definition.
- Role boundaries and exclusion boundaries.
- Ethical orientation and operating posture.
- Decision profile expected at graduation.

### Governance Rule
School design must conform to the approved Professional Identity Framework.

## 4. Professional Standards

### Requirement
Each school must define explicit standards for quality, integrity, reliability, and conduct.

### Mandatory Standard Domains
- Accuracy and output quality.
- Evidence and traceability integrity.
- Process discipline and governance compliance.
- Ethical and professional conduct.
- Risk management and safe operation.

### Governance Rule
Standards must be objective and enforceable through evaluation artifacts.

## 5. Programs

### Requirement
Each school must define the program architecture that structures learning pathways.

### Required Program Components
- Program tracks (if multiple).
- Entry criteria and prerequisites.
- Required progression sequence.
- Program-level stage gates.
- Program completion conditions.

### Governance Rule
Programs must map to declared professional outcomes and capability requirements.

## 6. Capabilities

### Requirement
Each school must specify the professional capabilities required for mission readiness.

### Capability Design Rules
- Capabilities must be mission-linked.
- Capability boundaries must be explicit.
- Dependencies and failure consequences must be documented.
- Capability maturity target must be declared.

### Governance Rule
Capabilities must map to APCF layer logic and ACF measurement architecture.

## 7. Competencies

### Requirement
Each capability must decompose into measurable competencies.

### Competency Architecture Requirements
- Competency statements must be behaviorally specific.
- Competencies must include objective evidence requirements.
- Progression levels must align with ACF.
- Critical competencies must define remediation pathways.

### Governance Rule
No competency may be approved without measurable evaluation criteria.

## 8. Curriculum Structure

### Requirement
Each school must define a curriculum structure that operationalizes capability and competency progression.

### Required Curriculum Components
- Module hierarchy.
- Objective mapping per module.
- Sequencing logic and dependency map.
- Assessment architecture.
- Capstone integration.

### Governance Rule
Curriculum design must remain subordinate to AEP philosophy, ATM methodology, and ACF competency rules.

## 9. Lessons

### Requirement
Each module must contain lesson structures that support competency development.

### Required Lesson Elements
- Lesson objective and expected behavior change.
- Instructional method selection rationale.
- Required inputs/resources.
- Evidence artifact outputs.
- Lesson-level evaluation criteria.

### Governance Rule
Lesson completion does not imply competency unless linked evaluation evidence meets threshold.

## 10. Practical Exercises

### Requirement
Each school must define practical exercises that convert conceptual understanding into operational behavior.

### Practical Exercise Standards
- Exercises must map to explicit competencies.
- Exercises must include realism constraints.
- Exercises must produce auditable performance artifacts.
- Exercise difficulty must follow staged progression.

### Governance Rule
Practical exercises are mandatory and cannot be replaced by theory-only alternatives.

## 11. Production Missions

### Requirement
Each school must include production-representative or live mission validation stages.

### Production Mission Requirements
- Mission objective aligned to professional role.
- Realistic uncertainty, constraints, and dependencies.
- Independent quality and risk review.
- Output utility confirmation by mission stakeholders.

### Governance Rule
Schools cannot certify professional readiness without production mission validation evidence.

## 12. Graduation Requirements

### Requirement
Each school must define graduation as an evidence-governed decision, not a completion event.

### Mandatory Graduation Gates
1. Critical competencies at required level.
2. Quality standards consistently met.
3. External evaluation free of unresolved critical findings.
4. Production mission validation passed.
5. AEIS evidence records complete and auditable.

### Graduation Outcomes
- Pass.
- Conditional Pass.
- Fail.

### Governance Rule
No credential may be issued without formal gate review and approval record.

## 13. AEIS Feedback Integration

### Requirement
Each school must integrate AEIS as its educational intelligence and governance instrumentation layer.

### AEIS Integration Requirements
- Record objective progression and evaluation outcomes.
- Capture quality, drift, and risk signals.
- Maintain graduation evidence completeness.
- Feed institutional analytics for cross-school comparison.

### Governance Rule
School performance claims are provisional without AEIS-backed evidence integrity.

## 14. Continuous Curriculum Improvement

### Requirement
Each school must run a formal continuous improvement cycle.

### Improvement Cycle Requirements
1. Collect outcome, mission, and evaluation data.
2. Diagnose systemic weaknesses and recurring failures.
3. Prioritize improvements by mission impact.
4. Update curriculum, exercises, and assessment controls.
5. Validate updates through pilots.
6. Institutionalize approved changes with version history.

### Governance Rule
Major curriculum updates require governance approval and impact traceability.

## 15. Relationship To AEP

AEP defines the constitutional educational philosophy for Atlas Academy.

Relationship rules:
- APSB-conformant schools must inherit AEP principles without dilution.
- If school design conflicts with AEP constitutional principles, AEP prevails.
- School governance documents must explicitly reference AEP alignment.

## 16. Relationship To ATM

ATM defines how learning is operationally conducted across schools.

Relationship rules:
- School delivery methods must implement ATM cycle and controls.
- Lesson, practice, critique, reflection, and validation mechanisms must align with ATM.
- Method deviations require formal governance exception approval.

## 17. Relationship To ACF

ACF defines how competency is evaluated, graduated, and recertified.

Relationship rules:
- School competency architecture must map directly to ACF dimensions.
- Graduation gates must satisfy ACF evidence requirements.
- Regression and recertification provisions must align to ACF standards.

## 18. Relationship To The Atlas Academy Governance Hierarchy

### Governance Hierarchy Alignment
Every professional school must align to the Atlas Academy hierarchy of authority:
1. AEP (constitutional educational philosophy).
2. ACF (constitutional competency architecture).
3. ATM (universal teaching method).
4. APCF and related capability governance frameworks.
5. APSB-conformant school governance documents.
6. School-specific curricula, assessments, and operational artifacts.

### Hierarchy Rule
Lower-level school artifacts cannot override higher-order constitutional and governance standards.

### Escalation Rule
Conflicts unresolved at school governance level must escalate through Academy governance channels for constitutional determination.

## School Lifecycle Template (Reusable)

All schools should follow this baseline lifecycle:
1. Charter: define purpose, mission, identity, and standards.
2. Architect: design programs, capabilities, competencies, and curriculum.
3. Validate: test lessons, exercises, and mission gates.
4. Launch: run controlled cohorts with AEIS instrumentation.
5. Certify: conduct evidence-based graduation decisions.
6. Operate: deliver ongoing cohorts with quality controls.
7. Improve: execute continuous refinement and version governance.
8. Reauthorize: periodic governance review for continued academy alignment.

## Adoption And Compliance

APSB is mandatory for all current and future Atlas Academy professional schools.

Compliance requirements:
1. Every school must produce a governance charter using APSB structure.
2. Every section in this blueprint is required.
3. Non-compliant schools are ineligible for Atlas Academy certification authority.
4. Exceptions require formal governance approval and recorded rationale.
