# Midnight Archives Brand Profile

## Business Name
Midnight Archives

## Mission
Produce cinematic short-form horror stories that prioritize narrative tension, emotional payoff, and high production discipline.

## Audience
- Primary: Adults 18-34 interested in horror storytelling, suspense, and visual mood pieces.
- Secondary: General short-form viewers who value strong hooks and cinematic pacing.

## Content Pillars
1. Atmospheric Horror: dread-driven scenes with controlled escalation.
2. Narrative Twist: meaningful final turn that recontextualizes events.
3. Character Stakes: emotionally grounded protagonists and consequences.
4. Mythic Fragments: recurring universe elements that reward repeat viewing.

## Visual Identity
- Palette: low-key contrast, deep shadows, selective accent lighting.
- Composition: cinematic framing, strong silhouette moments, scene clarity over noise.
- Motion language: deliberate pacing, visual continuity, no chaotic transitions.

## Narration Style
- Tone: calm-to-intense progression, controlled urgency, story-first delivery.
- Cadence: concise phrasing, strategic pauses, emphasis on dramatic beats.
- Voice personality: credible storyteller with emotional restraint.

## Thumbnail Style
- Single dominant focal subject.
- High-contrast title-safe composition.
- Minimal text, maximum emotional signal.
- Distinct visual mystery without plot spoilers.

## Publishing Cadence
- Baseline: 3 episodes per week.
- Cadence model: Mon/Wed/Fri production targets, private verification first.
- Escalation rule: quality block overrides cadence pressure.

## Quality Standards
1. Hook appears in first 3-5 seconds.
2. Narrative coherence from opening to final beat.
3. No unresolved technical defects in final media package.
4. Quality decision must pass before release candidate progression.
5. Executive and CEO approval paths remain enforced without exception.
